## Federal Funding Dependency Varies Substantially by Research Field at R1 Universities

Federal funding dominates R&D expenditures at R1 universities, but aggregate trends obscure substantial variation across scientific disciplines. This analysis uses the NSF Higher Education Research and Development (HERD) Survey (1974--2024) to examine federal share of total R&D expenditures across 24 science and engineering sub-fields over five decades. Federal dependency peaked across all fields in 1979 -- the tail end of Cold War research investment -- and has declined since, but the rate and floor of decline differ markedly by discipline. Physical sciences remain the most federally dependent; social sciences and agricultural sciences the least. Nonfederal source composition, R&D type shifts, and business investment patterns reveal that federal dollars are moving toward applied research faster than nonfederal dollars, while industry investment is growing only in the fields already most commercially oriented -- leaving no compensating source for basic research in high-dependency fields. ARIMA forecasts project continued decline through 2034 for most fields, with social sciences and life sciences holding relatively flat.
